Scoring Logic

The site is designed to explain how a response becomes a priority.

This page focuses only on the logic chain: what happens after a respondent chooses a maturity score, how targets are used, and how the final dashboard should be read.

Keep this principle in mind: low scores are not enough on their own.

The portal highlights weighted gaps to target so it can distinguish weak areas from truly urgent issues.

From Answer To Action

A four-step chain with no hidden logic.

Respond

Each question is scored against a maturity anchor and can be supported with evidence notes.

Aggregate

Scores roll upward into averages by pillar, by stage, and by enabler.

Compare

Actual maturity is read against target maturity so the system can calculate gaps.

Prioritize

Weighted gap-to-target logic ranks the questions that matter most for action planning.

How To Read The Dashboard

Three views answer three different management questions.

By Pillar

Which commercial lever is most exposed across Pricing, OBPPC, Promotion Spend, and DFR / Trade Investment?

By Stage

Where does the operating chain break: diagnosis, strategy, policy, execution, or sustainability?

By Enabler

Which enabling condition needs strengthening: governance, tools, people capability, or future readiness?

Priority Logic

The most useful output is the ranked action list, not the raw table.

Priority Principle Priority Index = Question Weight x Max(0, Target - Actual Score)

That means a low score only matters if it is tied to a meaningful target gap and a materially important question.

Low score, low weight

May indicate improvement need, but not necessarily immediate urgency.

High gap, high weight

Becomes a top roadmap candidate because it blocks enterprise capability more materially.

Consistent gaps by stage or enabler

Signal a systemic issue, not just a weak individual answer.
